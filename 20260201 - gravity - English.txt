For a long time, gravity was described using the framework developed by Isaac

The larger the masses, the stronger the attraction.

As scientific understanding advanced, this limitation became increasingly

Rather than treating it as a force acting across space, Einstein described

To an observer embedded within spacetime, this motion appears as acceleration.

From the inside, it feels exactly like a force acting downward.

This idea can feel abstract at first because curved spacetime is not something
that can be directly seen or touched, but its effects are measurable and

Clocks tick at different rates depending on their position in a gravitational

and slightly faster farther away.

This effect has been measured repeatedly using atomic clocks and must be
accounted for in satellite navigation systems to maintain accuracy.
